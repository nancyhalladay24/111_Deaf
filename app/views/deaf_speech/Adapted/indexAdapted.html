Get help in another language

If reading English is hard for you, call 111. The 111 phone service can get an interpreter to help you.

Using this service if you're deaf, hard of hearing or have speech problems.
111 telephone service

A British Sign Language (BSL) interpreter is available from 8am to midnight every day at interpreternow.co.uk/nhs111

You can contact 111 through text relay 24 hours a day. Call 18001 111 on a textphone or the text relay app.

111 online

You can use an adapted triage for hearing and speech impairments.

If a nurse does need to speak to you:

  • text relay can be used on an app or textphone
  • a BSL interpreter can be used if you need one

If you're told to contact 999:

  • 18000 is the text relay number for help in an emergency.
