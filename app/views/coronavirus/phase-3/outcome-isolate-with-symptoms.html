You need to stay at home

7 days

Lots of questions about what happens .. if? Majority of people get better.

If you’re on public transport, go home by the most direct route. Stay at least 2 metres away from people if you can.

If you’re away from home:

  • find a room where you can close the door
  • avoid touching people, surfaces and objects

To protect yourself and others, do not go to a GP, pharmacy or hospital

Manage mild symptoms at home and wait

If your symptoms are mild, for example you do not have:

  • shortness of breath
  • a cough that is bad enough to affect your quality of life
  • a temperature that doesn’t come down with paracetamol or ibuprofen

You can look after yourself at home as you would do with normal flu.

You will need to speak to 111 about getting tested, but it’s not urgent that you do that right now.

You should keep yourself separate from others in your household (self-isolate) until you have been spoken to 111. Read more details about self-isolation.

The phone service is currently very busy during the evenings and weekends, so try calling during the day instead. When you call, tell them you’ve been told you need a coronavirus test by the online service.

To manage your symptoms while you wait you can:

  • take paracetamol
  • drink plenty of water
  • get lots of rest

What to do if you feel very unwell

Call NHS 111 now. The phone service will be very busy, but you must wait until you get through.

Tell 111:

  • that you are calling about coronavirus (COVID-19) symptoms
  • where you have travelled or about any contact you’ve had with someone with symptoms

If you hear a recorded message, make sure you select the option to speak to someone.
