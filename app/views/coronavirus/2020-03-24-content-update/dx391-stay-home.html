You and your family need to self-isolate

To protect others, do not go to a GP, pharmacy or hospital

If you have a high temperature and other symptoms not related to cold or flu, check them using 111 online again.

Testing is not needed if you are self-isolating.

To avoid spreading infection

If you live alone you must self-isolate for 7 days

Do not leave your home for 7 days from when your symptoms started, however mild they are.

This means you must not leave your home for any reason, other than to exercise once a day – but stay at least 2 metres (3 steps) away from other people.

If your temperature is still high after 7 days, stay at home until it’s back to normal.

If you live with other people, they should self-isolate for 14 days

Although you need only self-isolate for 7 days, other people you live with should stay in for 14 days.

If more than 1 person at home has symptoms, self-isolate for 14 days from the day the first person started having symptoms.

Vulnerable people

Minimise as much as possible the time any vulnerable family members spend in shared spaces such as kitchens, bathrooms and sitting areas and keep shared spaces well ventilated.

Read more self-isolation advice on NHS.UK

Get an isolation note to give to your employer

You can send an isolation note to your employer as proof you need to stay off work because of coronavirus.

You do not need to get a note from a GP.

How to look after your symptoms at home

  • rest and drink plenty of fluids
  • take paracetamol
  • cover your mouth with a tissue when coughing or sneezing
  • put used tissues in a bin
  • wash your hands regularly with warm water and soap for at least 20 seconds

If you feel you can’t cope with your symptoms at home, or your condition gets worse, use the 111 online service again.
