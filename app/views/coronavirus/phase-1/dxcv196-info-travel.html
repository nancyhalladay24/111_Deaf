You don’t need to speak to anybody right now as you:

  • have not been to a country with a serious outbreak
  • have not been in contact with a confirmed case
  • are not showing any symptoms

Call 111 if you start to develop symptoms of:

  • shortness of breath
  • high temperature (feeling hot to touch on the chest or tummy)
  • a cough

Make sure you select the option to speak to someone. Tell them you are calling about possible coronavirus (COVID-19) symptoms.

The phone service will be very busy but you must wait until you get through.

Information for people who work in healthcare settings

Healthcare workers include clinical, admin and social care staff.

You can continue to go to work as normal if you have no symptoms, unless you have been in any healthcare setting in the country you visited, in which case you should self-isolate for 14 days.

What is self-isolation?

This means you should:

  • stay at home
  • not go to work, school or public places
  • not use public transport or taxis
  • ask friends, family members or delivery services to carry out errands for you
  • try to avoid visitors to your home – it's OK for friends, family or delivery drivers to drop off food

This is to help reduce the possible spread of infection.

If you develop symptoms you must tell your line manager and your occupational health department, as well as calling 111.

You can read advice and find answers to common questions about coronavirus on NHS.UK.
