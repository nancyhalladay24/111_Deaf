You and the people you live with should not leave your home for any reason

Your symptoms suggest that you could have a coronavirus infection. You should get tested as soon as you can.

How long to isolate for and managing symptoms at home

Self-isolation is for people with symptoms. It is different to staying at home and social distancing, which is for everyone.

NHS.UK has information to help if you have symptoms. You can find out:

  • how long to isolate for if you or someone you live with has symptoms
  • how to look after yourself and manage your symptoms at home

Get tested for coronavirus

The test will show whether you have the virus now.

It’s done by either:

  • going to a drive-through testing centre if you have a car (you can’t get a taxi)
  • getting a home test kit sent to you

Key workers are being given priority at the moment, but anyone with symptoms can apply. If you can’t get one straightaway you should continue to check.

Check if you can get tested

Other things you can do on NHS.UK

You can also:

  • get an isolation note (like a sick note) to give to your employer if you need time off work
  • help the NHS plan its response to coronavirus by telling us about your current experience of the virus
  • share your mobile number to get advice and links to trusted sources of information and support
