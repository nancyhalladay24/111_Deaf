You and the people you live with should not leave your home for any reason

Your symptoms suggest that you could have a coronavirus infection.

Do not leave your home if you have symptoms or live with someone who does.

This is called self-isolation. Self-isolation is for people with symptoms. It is different to staying at home and social distancing, which is for everyone.

If you are self-isolating, you must:

  • not have visitors, such as friends and family, in your home

You can use your garden, if you have one. Any exercise should be done at home.

How long to isolate for and managing symptoms at home

NHS.UK has information to help if you have symptoms. You can find out:

  • how long to isolate for if you or someone you live with has symptoms
  • how to look after yourself and manage your symptoms at home

Coronavirus testing

You can register your details on the home testing website to be sent a coronavirus test kit.

The test kit information will explain how to carry out the simple swab test yourself. You then return it to be analysed and wait for the result.

Register your details

Other things you can do on NHS.UK:

  • get an isolation note (like a sick note) to give to your employer if you need time off work
  • help the NHS plan its response to coronavirus by telling us about your current experience of the virus
  • share your mobile number to get advice and links to trusted sources of information and support
